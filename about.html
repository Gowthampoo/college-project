Our Institution

The first centre of higher education in Bantwal Taluk — nestled among hillocks, committed to affordable and quality education for over 5 decades.

A Legacy Since 1968

Sri Venkataramana Swamy College, Bantwal was founded by Late Sri Bantwal Raghurama Mukunda Prabhu, a great visionary, along with a group of philanthropists, with the sole objective of catering to the educational needs of young students in this backward region.

The trust was registered as Sri Venkataramana Swamy Vidyavardhaka Sangha in 1966, and the college was born on 1st April 1968 — starting with just 140 students and two programmes: B.A. and B.Com.

Nestled among a cluster of hillocks in serene surroundings, the institution is blissfully away from the din and bustle of city life, offering a peaceful and focused environment for learning.

Our Journey
Key milestones in our history
1966

Trust Registered

Sri Venkataramana Swamy Vidyavardhaka Sangha was registered by philanthropists of Bantwal.

1968

College Founded

The college started functioning with 140 students, offering B.A. and B.Com — the first centre for higher education in Bantwal Taluk.

1981

UGC Recognition

Recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) under Section 2(f) on 26th May 1981.

2000s

Expansion of Courses

Introduction of B.Sc. Computer Science, B.B.M., B.C.A., and M.Com courses.

2016
